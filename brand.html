01 / Logo system

Marks, lockups, and monogram.

The Grimnir sigil is a single geometric hood with a luminous horizontal slit. The slit is the face of our brand: it watches, it never blinks. Use the primary mark for social avatars and favicons. Reach for the lockups anywhere text and mark appear together.

04 / Clearspace & size

Give the hood air.

The Grimnir mark is built on a 4-unit grid. Maintain clearspace equal to the height of the eye slit on every side. The mark is never set below 24 px on screen or 0.3 inches in print.

Clearspace rule

Minimum clearspace around the mark on all four sides is equal to the height of the eye slit.

Minimum sizes

Below these sizes, drop to the monogram or favicon variant.

05 / Do & don't

Keep the mark sharp.

The hood is a system, not a prop. Follow these rules or skip the mark entirely.

Do

Use the primary mark on dark surfaces. Let the slit glow.

Do

Use the dark monochrome variant on light backgrounds for print.

Do

Pair mark and wordmark with a lockup. Never recreate the spacing.

Don't

Do not recolor, tint, or gradient-shift the mark outside the palette.

Don't

Do not rotate, skew, mirror, or distort the hood silhouette.

Don't

Don't
GRIMNIR

06 / Voice & tone

Cool. Quiet. Cutting.

Grimnir talks like a watcher on a rooftop, not a hype-driven newsroom. Short sentences. No jargon that isn't load-bearing. No exclamation marks. No emoji in editorial voice.

Axis 01
Confident, not loud.

State what is known. Hedge only with data. Never beg for attention.

Axis 02
Terse, not terse-ironic.

Cut words. Keep the thought whole. Dry wit is fine. Sarcasm is not.

Axis 03
Factual, not clickbait.

Headlines describe the event. They never tease it. No "You won't believe."

Axis 04
Dark, not edgelord.

Shadow is an aesthetic, not a worldview. Skip cruelty. Keep the hood cool.

On brand

"Ransomware group claims 40 TB from logistics firm. Source unverified."

Off brand

"MASSIVE BREACH!! 40TB STOLEN - you NEED to see this now!!"

On brand

"CISA adds three kernel CVEs to KEV catalog. Patches live upstream."
